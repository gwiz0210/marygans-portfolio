<h3><strong>Overview</strong></h3><p>Catalog AI is an internal Microsoft platform designed to help sellers quickly find and recommend the best delivery options for customer opportunities. By inputting an opportunity ID, sellers receive tailored results that surface <strong>qualifying entitlements</strong>, <strong>for-fee delivery options</strong>, and <strong>recommended partners</strong>, all powered by AI and organized for fast decision-making.</p><p>‍</p><h3><strong>Challenge</strong></h3><p>Sellers previously had to navigate multiple systems and fragmented information sources to recommend the right delivery option, leading to inefficiencies and inconsistent customer experiences.</p><p>‍</p><h3><strong>Solution</strong></h3><p>I designed the results page experience that unifies AI-generated delivery recommendations into three clear sections:</p><ol><li><strong>Qualifying Entitlements</strong> – No-cost offerings customers are eligible for.</li><li><strong>For-Fee Delivery Options</strong> – Paid services with transparent pricing, delivery time, and adoption metrics.</li><li><strong>Recommended Partners</strong> – Vetted delivery partners, filterable by region and specialization.</li></ol><p>The design emphasized <strong>clarity, actionability, and trust</strong>, with consistent card layouts, clear CTAs (“Learn More” / “View Partner”), and metadata for easy comparison.</p><p>‍</p><h3><strong>Results</strong></h3><ul><li>Reduced time to identify delivery options from <strong>10+ minutes to under 2 minutes</strong>.</li><li>Increased seller adoption through intuitive navigation and consolidated information.</li><li>Created a scalable card framework that can support additional recommendation types in the future.</li></ul><p>‍</p><h3><strong>Research &amp; Design Process</strong></h3><ul><li>Partnered with PM and engineering to map seller workflows and prioritize the most common use cases.</li><li>Iterated on wireframes and interactive prototypes based on stakeholder feedback.</li><li>Defined card layouts that balanced information density with quick scannability.</li><li>Validated with sellers to ensure the AI recommendations were easy to interpret and act upon.</li></ul><p>‍</p><h3><strong>Learnings</strong></h3><ul><li>Sellers value <strong>credibility signals</strong> (number sold, delivery time, cost tiers) when making recommendations.</li><li>Grouping recommendations by <strong>type and intent</strong> reduces cognitive load and speeds decision-making.</li><li>Early AI-generated results must still provide <strong>human-readable context</strong> to build trust.</li></ul><p>‍</p>
